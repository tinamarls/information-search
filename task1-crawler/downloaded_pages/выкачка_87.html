Коллеги! Помните, что разница между всеми проектами на заглавной странице и «Знаете ли вы» похожа на разницу между научными и научно-популярными журналами. Научные журналы увеличивают сумму знаний, научпоп призван развлечь и заинтересовать читателей. Пожалуйста:

  • Не будьте Прокрустом, разрешите избирающим некоторую энциклопедическую вольность.

  • Полезно перечитать ВП:ПРОТЕСТ.

    • Early Context: In the 17th century, men initially played all roles, including female ones, due to restrictions on women performers. Jean-Baptiste Lully's ballets, such as Le Triomphe de l'Amour (1681), marked the introduction of professional female dancers, though not yet in travesty roles.
    • 18th-Century Developments: By the 1730s, women began taking male roles, often in comedic or youthful parts. Marie Sallé, a pioneering French ballerina, experimented with expressive roles and may have performed in male attire, though specific ballets are not definitively recorded. Michel Blondy, a choreographer at the Paris Opera Ballet, collaborated on works like Les Indes Galantes (1735), where female dancers occasionally portrayed male characters.
    • Institutionalization in the 19th Century: The Romantic era solidified travesty roles, with ballerinas like Fanny Elssler performing male characters. Jules Perrot's La Esmeralda (1844) featured women en travesti, such as the role of Quasimodo.— Victoria (обс.) 10:22, 3 марта 2025 (UTC)[ответить]
